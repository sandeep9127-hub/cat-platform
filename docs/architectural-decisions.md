# Architectural decisions

Locked decisions for the CAT Platform, as of 20 May 2026. Each entry names the question,
the call we made, and why. Reverse only with an editor-team conversation.

## 1. Lead with a list, not with one entry

**Decision.** The home page leads with a list view of CAT Landscapes and the Solutions
Atlas, not with a single rotating "featured entry."

**Why.** A featured entry is a magazine pattern. It teaches readers to expect a curated
hero and skim the rest. We want readers to land knowing this is a structured index, not a
campaign site. The list also makes editorial sparseness visible: an empty landscape reads
as honest, where a hero would hide it.

**What this rules out.** Editor's picks, rotating tiles, carousel heroes, "trending"
slots.

## 2. Atlas is co-equal with CAT Landscapes, not demoted

**Decision.** The Solutions Atlas (open programmes from across India) sits beside the CAT
Landscapes (closed CAT-only set), with equal visual weight in navigation, search, and the
map.

**Why.** Demoting the Atlas would turn the Platform into a CAT promotional site. Treating
them as co-equal forces editors to defend why a CAT entry is included on its own merit,
not on its parentage. Funders explicitly asked for cross-portfolio comparison; demotion
breaks that.

**What this rules out.** Atlas-as-appendix, "external resources" treatment, separate
visual language that signals lower trust.

## 3. "What did not work" is the fifth section, not the first

**Decision.** Every published entry carries five sections in fixed order: Context, What
is being done, What is working, **What is not working**, What we are watching next.
"What did not work" is required infrastructure, not an optional footnote, and not the
opening.

**Why.** Leading with failure performs honesty rather than practising it. Burying it as
optional protects the writer at the cost of the reader. Fifth-position is the discipline:
you have to write through the achievement before naming the limitation, which forces
specificity in both.

**What this rules out.** Optional "challenges" toggles. "Lessons learned" sidebars that
get dropped when an entry is short. Failure-first formats borrowed from the engineering
post-mortem tradition.
