## Email body

Hello {{first_name}},

A short note to introduce the **Transformation Hub** ([cat-platform-fawn.vercel.app](https://cat-platform-fawn.vercel.app)), a public, curated dashboard of credible food systems work in India.

The Hub is built and edited by the Consortium for Agroecological Transformations (CAT). It covers programmes nationally, not only CAT's portfolio. Government missions, NGO programmes, federations, market infrastructure, policy experiments. The bar is honesty, not affiliation.

**What sits on the Hub today**

- **11 CAT Landscapes** with full editorial profiles, sourced from the official Feb 2026 fact sheets. The Patratu landscape carries the full investment plan: library of source documents, line-item budget explorer, and a scoped AI assistant that answers questions from the ingested LIP.
- **A Solutions Atlas** of programmes from across India, organised by theme and region, with the "what did not work" section sitting beside "what worked".
- **A library** of reports, briefs, and datasets, citable by URL.
- **An AI assistant** that reads the published library only, cites the entries it draws from, and refuses to invent.
- **Per-landscape downloads**: every profile is available as a one-page PDF or DOCX brief, generated from the same source data.

**A few things to know**

- Every entry is read by a CAT editor before it goes live. Three transparency tiers (Authored, Endorsed, Listed) tell you how much editorial weight is behind each entry.
- No registration. Funders, programme officers, researchers, and journalists can read everything by URL. Every filter and search state is a shareable link.
- No tracking cookies, no third-party analytics that profile you, no ads.

**What I would ask**

If you have ten minutes, the two places to start are the home page and any one landscape profile (Patratu is the most complete). If a programme you fund or run is missing from the Atlas, the contribute page takes a paragraph plus two source links. If a published entry reads off-tone or off-fact, please write back to me.

Happy to walk through any of this on a call.

With regards,

**Sandeep Nayak**
Programme Lead, Food Systems
Consortium for Agroecological Transformations
