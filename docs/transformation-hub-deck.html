The brief 02 · 13
In one sentence

An editorial dashboard that documents what is being done about food systems in India, and what is not working alongside what is.

The Hub is the public-facing publication of CAT. It covers programmes nationally, not just CAT's portfolio. Government missions, NGO programmes, federations, market infrastructure, policy experiments.

Stance 03 · 13
What this is, and what this is not

It is

  • 01.A reading surface for funders, programme designers, and researchers.
  • 02.An AI-assisted editorial publication. People decide, AI scales production.
  • 03.A defensible record of what was attempted, what worked, what did not.
  • 04.Open, link-driven, no registration to read.

It is not

  • 01.A project-management tool. CAT does not run operations from here.
  • 02.A GIS platform. The map is editorial illustration, never survey-grade.
  • 03.A CAT marketing site. CAT is the editor, not the subject.
  • 04.A volume directory. Curation is the bar, scale is not the win.

Feature · Assistant 07 · 13
Ask the Hub

A scoped AI assistant that reads only the published library and cites every entry it draws from.

Route aware

On a landscape page, the assistant scopes to that landscape's library. Elsewhere it draws from the full library.

Refuses to invent

If the context is thin, it says so and suggests related themes. No hallucinated programmes.

Floating, keyboard-first

Hover-pill at bottom-right of every reading page. Cmd or Ctrl + K opens. Esc closes.

Transparent

Citation chips link back to the source entry. The user always sees what the assistant read.

Standards 08 · 13
Three editorial tiers, shown on every entry

Authored, Endorsed, Listed. The label tells you how much we vouch.

CAT AUTHORED

Wholly read and written by CAT editors.

We vouch for the reading and the framing. The highest editorial weight on the Hub.

CAT ENDORSED

Written by a partner. Edited and verified by CAT.

We stand behind the description as published; the framing came from the implementer.

CAT LISTED

The programme exists and is documented.

CAT has not done deep editorial work yet. Useful for cross-referencing.

Every published entry must carry five sections in fixed order. Context, what is being done, what is working, what is not working, what we are watching next.

Voice 09 · 13
How the Hub writes

“Plain language. Short sentences. Specifics over abstractions. We write as if briefing a thoughtful funder over coffee, not pitching a panel.”

From the Hub style guide
No em dashes

Anywhere. We use commas, full stops, parentheses, colons.

No marketing words

No “leverage”, “stakeholder”, “ecosystem”, “transformative”.

Indian English

Organise, programme, centre. Indian numerals on budgets and populations.

Trust 10 · 13
What we store, what we do not

No tracking, no ads, no funnels. Open by default.

Funders never register

Every filter, search state, and entry is a shareable URL. Senior readers can land from a meeting and find what they need in under a minute.

No tracking cookies

Aggregate page-view counts only, via Vercel Analytics. No Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Hotjar. The one functional cookie is the session cookie after a contributor signs in.

Editorial right protected

Funders cannot request the removal of a “what did not work” section from any entry. The independence rule is published, not assumed.

Removal on request

Contributors can remove their account, submissions, and any agent-conversation records linked to their session by writing to editors@cat.org.in.

Under the hood 11 · 13
A modest, dependable stack

Built on Next.js, Postgres with pgvector, hosted on Vercel.

Reading layer

Next.js 15 App Router · TypeScript strict · Tailwind v3 with brand tokens.

Data layer

Drizzle ORM on Postgres in an isolated cat schema · pgvector for RAG search · Supabase Postgres in ap-south-1 (Mumbai).

AI layer

Kimi K2.6 served via NVIDIA's hosted API for assistant calls. nv-embedqa-e5-v5 for 1024-dim chunk embeddings. Anthropic Claude Sonnet for discovery agent calls with web search.

Editorial pipeline

DOCX and XLSX intake, chunked and embedded server-side, indexed to pgvector with an HNSW cosine index.

Audience 12 · 13
Designed for, in this order

Senior readers who arrive from a meeting, not a feed.

  • 01.Funders and their advisors. Quick-glance, link-driven. Will spend 3 to 8 minutes on a single entry.
  • 02.NGOs and programme designers. Read deeply. Care about “what did not work” more than headlines.
  • 03.Researchers, students, journalists. Follow citations, share URLs, request downloads.
  • 04.Government and policy actors. Light usage, occasional deep dive on state-mission overlaps.
